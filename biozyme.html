Biozyme is a biofertilizer brand whose last major brand campaign was from the 1990s. The brief was simple and enormous: bring the brand into the 2020s.

Three decades is a long time for any brand to stand still. Biozyme hadn't. The product had evolved, the science had improved, the market had changed. But the brand's voice hadn't kept up.

The rebrand wasn't about making the brand look modern — it was about making it feel relevant. Biofertilizers are genuinely important: better for soil, better for crops, better for the planet. The film found the human truth behind the science and built a brand around it.

A brand that was decades behind, brought forward in one film.

Brand Film

"Thirty years behind. One film forward."

The hardest rebrand brief isn't "make us look different." It's "make people feel something they've never felt about this brand before." Biozyme needed a voice, a face, and a reason to exist in a world that had moved on without it. The film gave it all three.
