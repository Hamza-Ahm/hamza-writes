Two IKEA campaigns built around the same core truth: the home is where life actually happens. Home Improver showed what's possible with small changes. Better Together showed what home means when you share it.

The Home Improver series took specific, relatable home problems — a badly lit wall, a cluttered room, a dull corner — and showed the transformation IKEA makes possible. Not aspirational. Achievable.

Better Together leaned into the emotional dimension — home as a space shaped by the people in it. The film found warmth without sentimentality, which is the hardest thing to do in home retail.

The Home Improver films worked because they started with a problem anyone could recognise — and showed the solution without making it feel like an ad. The Better Together film worked because it found warmth without reaching for it. Both are harder than they look.
